<p class="mt-0">Prospective students interested in applying to the Robotics Graduate Program must submit a Rackham Graduate School [online application](https://rackham.umich.edu/admissions/applying/).</p>

## Application fees and payment

Rackham Graduate School [lists required fees and waiver](https://rackham.umich.edu/admissions/applying/application-fee-and-waivers/) information, which includes:

* United States citizen & permanent resident: $75  
* Non-Resident citizen: $90  
* Current University of Michigan (U-M) Ann Arbor Rackham graduate student: $10  
* The application fee is non-refundable and subject to change.  
* All prospective students pay the application fee in order for their application to be processed and submitted to Robotics, unless they received an approved Rackham application fee waiver or a need-based fee grant.

**Fee Waivers**: The Robotics Program does not provide fee waivers or grants. For information about fee waivers and need-based grants for Ph.D. and MSE applicants, see the [Rackham website](https://rackham.umich.edu/admissions/applying/application-fee-and-waivers/). 

**Enrollment Deposit**: An enrollment deposit is not required.

## What application to submit

Michigan Robotics offers two graduate degrees: **Master’s of Science (MS)** and a **PhD in Robotics**, however, there are several applications to choose from.

**PhD Application**
<br />This is for all applicants who will have completed at least a Bachelor’s degree by the beginning of their PhD program. 

**MS Application**
<br />This is for all applicants who will have completed at least a Bachelor’s degree by the beginning of their MS program and plan to complete their academic journey at the Master’s level. 

**SUGS**
<br />The [Sequential Undergraduate/Graduate Studies (SUGS) program](/academics/graduate/sugs/) is available only to students completing a Robotics BSE intending to complete the Robotics MS.

**Change of Program**
<br />This is for applicants who *are currently enrolled* in a Rackham graduate program and wish to [change from one graduate program to another Rackham graduate program](/academics/graduate/admissions/change-of-program/).

**Dual Degree**
<br />This is for applicants who are not currently in the Robotics program and are seeking to [add a Robotics Master’s Degree](/academics/graduate/admissions/dual-degree/). *Note: Applicants whose primary program offers a robotics option (e.g. ECE and MECHENG) will not be admitted.*

## After you submit your application….

### University of Michigan Identification (UMID) number

Applicants who apply to Robotics will receive a UMID number after submitting the online application to Rackham. See Rackham’s [Register for Your University of Michigan Identification Number (UMID) link](https://rackham.umich.edu/admissions/checklist-for-completing-the-application-process#create-umid) for more information. If you do not receive the UMID number in ten days from submitting the application, please contact Rackham directly at rackadmis@umich.edu.

### Application review

Your application will have multiple evaluations times, reviewed by trained College of Engineering application evaluators and by Robotics faculty. Unfortunately, we are unable to provide pre-application evaluations or feedback on the competitiveness of your application.

### Updating your application after submission

#### Personal Information

If information on your application is incorrect or incomplete, such as your date of birth, or your name, you can notify the Rackham Graduate School at [rackadmis@umich.edu](mailto:rackadmis@umich.edu) and request the correction. They will inform you of any required documents to confirm the new information and could include your UMID number, a copy of your US Driver’s License, a Passport, a marriage license, birth certificate, etc.

#### Additional materials or updates

Due to volume, unfortunately, we are unable to process requests for updates to your application such as revised essays, resume/CV, additional letters of recommendation, etc.

### How to check status of application

Before viewing whether your application and materials have been received you must:

1. Submit an application  
2. Receive a University of Michigan ID number (sent 5 business days after Rackham receives the application)  
3. Create a Friend Account, if you do not have a UMICH uniqname and Kerberos password  
4. Log into Wolverine Access and select New & Prospective Student Business.  
   Allow 10 business days, after completing the steps above, to view your application and materials.  

You can confirm the receipt of the following via Wolverine Access:  
* Test scores  
* Transcripts from institutions that awarded the Bachelor’s, Master’s, Professional, and/or Doctoral degree.  
* Recommendations can be tracked through the online application system, ApplyWeb Account, activity page, or in your Wolverine Access account.

All applicants with complete applications will receive a notification of their admission decision from the Robotics Department. Please refer to the deadlines chart on the Admissions page to understand when decisions are typically made by.
